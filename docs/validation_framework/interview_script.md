# User Interview Script (30-45 min)

## Pre-Interview
- [ ] Calendar invite sent with Zoom link
- [ ] Gift card ($25) ready to send
- [ ] Recording permission granted
- [ ] Notepad/doc ready

## Opening (5 min)
"Thanks for your time. I'm researching [problem space] to understand how people currently handle [situation]. This isn't a sales call - I genuinely want to learn about your experience. I'll record this for notes, is that okay?"

## The 5 Essential Questions

### 1. Last Time You Faced This (10 min)
"Tell me about the **last time** you encountered [problem]."
- Listen for: Frequency, context, stakes
- Follow-up: "Walk me through exactly what happened"
- Probe: "How did you feel when that occurred?"

### 2. Current Solutions (10 min)  
"What have you **tried** to solve this?"
- Listen for: Alternatives, workarounds, DIY efforts
- Follow-up: "How well did that work?"
- Probe: "What do you wish it did differently?"

### 3. Cost of Problem (5 min)
"What did this **cost** you in time or money?"
- Listen for: Quantified impact (hours, dollars, opportunities)
- Follow-up: "How often does this happen?"
- Probe: "What would fixing this be worth to you?"

### 4. Current Workflow (10 min)
"Walk me through your **current workflow** step-by-step."
- Listen for: Tools used, pain points, workarounds
- Follow-up: "Where do things typically break down?"
- Probe: "What takes the most time?"

### 5. Switching Criteria (5 min)
"What would need to be **true** for you to switch to a new solution?"
- Listen for: Objections, deal-breakers, must-haves
- Follow-up: "What would stop you from trying it?"
- Probe: "How much would you expect to pay?"

## Closing (5 min)
"This was incredibly helpful. Two final questions:
1. Is there anyone else I should talk to about this?
2. Can I reach out when we have something ready to test?"

Send gift card immediately after.
