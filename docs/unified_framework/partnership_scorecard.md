**4. Financial Contribution (__/10)**
- [ ] Can contribute $10K+ capital (10 pts)

**5. Cultural Fit (__/10)**

- [ ] Enjoyable to spend time with

**Decision Thresholds:**
- **40-50:** Strong candidate, proceed
- **30-39:** Conditional, address gaps

## ðŸ’° Equity Framework

### The Problem with 50/50

**Why 50/50 often fails:**
- Creates deadlock in decisions
- Assumes equal contribution (rarely true)
- Ignores different risk levels
- Doesn't account for idea origination
- Prevents future fundraising (who's CEO?)

### Evidence-Based Equity Split

**5-Factor Framework:**

**1. Idea (5-10%)**
- Originated concept: 10%
- Identified market opportunity: 5%
- Neither (joined existing): 0%

**2. Execution (25-35%)**
- Will be full-time from day 1: 35%
- Full-time after validation: 25%
- Part-time (<20 hours/week): 15%

**3. Capital (20-30%)**
- Investing $50K+: 30%
- Investing $20-50K: 20%
- Investing $5-20K: 10%
- No investment: 0%

**4. Risk (15-25%)**
- Quit job, no runway: 25%
- Quit job, has runway: 20%
- Side project while employed: 10%
- Consulting on side: 5%

**5. Domain Expertise (15-25%)**
- Deep expertise (10+ years): 25%
- Moderate expertise (5-10 years): 15%
- Learning on job: 5%
- No relevant experience: 0%

**Total: 100%**

## ðŸ“œ Vesting & Legal

### Standard Vesting Structure

**4-Year Vesting, 1-Year Cliff:**
- Nothing vests for first year
- 25% vests at 1 year
- Remaining 75% vests monthly (years 2-4)
- If someone leaves early, unvested shares return to company

**Why this matters:**
- Protects against early departures
- Aligns long-term commitment
- Standard for investors (won't fund without it)
- Fair to those who stay

**Example:**
```
Partner gets 30% equity with 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
â†’ Month 12: 7.5% vests
â†’ Month 24: 15% vests (total)
â†’ Month 36: 22.5% vests (total)
â†’ Month 48: 30% vests (total)

If they leave Month 18: They keep 11.25%, company keeps 18.75%
```

### Operating Agreement Essentials

**Must-haves:**
- [ ] Equity split clearly stated
- [ ] Vesting schedule defined
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities
- [ ] Decision-making authority (who decides what?)
- [ ] Tie-breaker mechanism (if 50/50)
- [ ] Buyout provisions (if someone wants out)
- [ ] IP assignment (company owns everything)
- [ ] Non-compete clause (1-2 years)
- [ ] Dispute resolution (mediation/arbitration)

**Get a lawyer:** Use Clerky ($500) or attorney ($1,500-3,000)

## ðŸ§ª Trial Period (Critical!)

### 3-Month Partnership Test

**Before committing equity:**

**Month 1: Paid Consulting ($5-10K)**
- Hire them as consultant
- Define specific deliverable
- See if they deliver
- Assess work style

**Month 2: Part-Time Collaboration**
- Work on real project together
- Weekly sync meetings
- See if communication works
- Check cultural fit

**Month 3: Decision Point**
- Review outcomes
- Re-run scorecard
- Get external feedback
- Make GO/NO-GO decision

**Only commit equity after successful trial.**

### Trial Questions to Answer

**Execution:**
- [ ] Do they ship on time?
- [ ] Is quality acceptable?
- [ ] Do they take initiative?
- [ ] How do they handle feedback?

**Communication:**
- [ ] Responsive (< 24 hours)?
- [ ] Clear and concise?
- [ ] Honest about challenges?
- [ ] Open to disagreement?

**Cultural:**
- [ ] Fun to work with?
- [ ] Similar work hours?
- [ ] Compatible decision-making?
- [ ] Handle stress well?

## ðŸ’” When to Part Ways

### Early-Stage (Before Equity Vesting)

**Clear indicators:**
- Not delivering on commitments
- Communication breakdown
- Values misalignment
- Better opportunity for them (and they're not committed)
- Your needs changed (skill gap different now)

**How to end it:**
- Honest conversation (respect)
- Fair financial settlement (if any money exchanged)
- Clean IP transfer (everything to company)
- Maintain relationship (industry is small)
